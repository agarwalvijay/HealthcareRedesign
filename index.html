The Grocery Store Paradox

"Imagine a grocery store with no prices on the shelves. You walk in, take what you need, and pay exactly $1 at the exit."

You don’t care whether the steak costs $5 or $500 — you aren’t the one paying the bill. A third party pays the store later, at whatever price the store decides to charge. Can such a world even exist?

This is not fiction. This is U.S. Healthcare.

By insulating patients from prices, we have removed the system’s natural cost discipline[cite: 347, 348]. The buyer has been separated from the transaction[cite: 348]. The result is a healthcare economy that cannot self-regulate[cite: 345, 590].

Redesign means putting the price back on the shelf — and the purchasing power back in the hands of patients[cite: 351, 355].
