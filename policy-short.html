Executive Brief

Restoring the Buyer

A summarized framework for Consumer-Anchored Healthcare Redesign.

The Core Problem

Healthcare is expensive not because of quality, but because of insulation. When third parties (insurers) pay the bills, providers stop competing on price.

  • Administrative Waste: Hospitals now spend $2 on administration for every $1 on patient care[cite: 3].
  • Price Gouging: A simple Echo costs $350 at a clinic but $4,500 at a hospital[cite: 59].
  • Consolidation: Hospitals buy doctors to corner the market, raising prices 14% overnight[cite: 73].

The Solution

We propose shifting from "defined benefits" (open-ended insurance) to "defined contributions" (funded accounts). Give the subsidy to the patient, not the insurance company.

Three Pillars of Reform

1. The "HSA Option"

Convert ACA subsidies into direct deposits. Instead of paying an insurer to lower your deductible, the government deposits that $2,000 directly into your Health Savings Account. It becomes your capital.

2. Site-Neutrality

A mandate that pays the same price for a service regardless of the building it happens in. This kills the incentive for hospitals to buy independent clinics.

3. The Dynamic Safety Net

A "Reverse Deductible." If a low-income patient has a chronic need (like insulin), the system automatically replenishes their HSA before they run out of funds.
